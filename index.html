The Brown Bear

About the Brown Bears

The brown bear(Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation status is currently Learst Concern.

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.


Wikipedia Reference for Brown Bears

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilis
  • Nelsoni(extinct)

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large, curved calws and huge paws. Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can range from 5 feet to 9 feet from head to toe.
